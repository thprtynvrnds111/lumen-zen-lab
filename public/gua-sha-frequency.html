How to use your Gua Sha Frequency

01

Prep

Apply facial oil or a water-based serum as a slip medium — the gua sha edge requires lubrication for effective gliding. Facial oils work here, unlike standard microcurrent use, because the scraping motion rather than electrical conductivity is the primary mechanism.

02

Sculpt

Hold the device at 15–45 degrees to the skin. Work in upward and outward strokes from the centre of the face. Neck → jawline → cheeks → forehead. 5–10 strokes per zone with light, consistent pressure.

03

Seal

The gua sha movement opens circulation and pores. Apply your serum immediately after and allow it to absorb fully before moisturiser. The red light and microcurrent activity elevates permeability for approximately 60 seconds post-session.

FAQ

Common questions

Does electric gua sha work better than stone gua sha?

The gua sha mechanism itself is similar — physical scraping to stimulate lymphatic drainage and circulation. The electric version adds microcurrent and red light, which stone tools cannot deliver, making it more effective for collagen stimulation and cellular repair alongside the drainage benefits.

Will it cause the redness traditional gua sha does?

The thermal assist and controlled electric motion allow effective technique with lighter pressure — significantly reducing the redness (petechiae) that aggressive stone gua sha can produce. The device is calibrated for regular daily use without irritation.

Can I use facial oil with it?

Yes — unlike other microcurrent devices, the Gua Sha Frequency works effectively with facial oils as the slip medium for the scraping motion. The microcurrent is delivered through the device surface rather than requiring a water-based conductor.

How often should I use it?

Daily use for the first 4–6 weeks for the most visible sculpting results, then 3–4 times per week for maintenance. Traditional gua sha practitioners recommend daily use precisely because the lymphatic benefit compounds with frequency.
