The science

What the technology
actually does

EMS — Electrical Muscle Stimulation

Muscle Activation & Toning

EMS delivers electrical impulses that cause involuntary muscle contractions — the same mechanism used in physiotherapy and professional sports recovery. On the face, this translates to tighter, more defined muscles under the skin: a sharper jawline, lifted cheeks, reduced sagging.

Microcurrent

Cellular Rejuvenation

Microcurrent operates at the cellular level, stimulating the production of ATP (adenosine triphosphate) — the energy that drives collagen and elastin synthesis. This is the mechanism behind the "natural facelift" effect: firmer skin, reduced fine lines, improved elasticity over time.

Thermal Technology

Deep Tissue Warming

Gentle thermal energy penetrates below the surface to stimulate circulation and collagen remodelling. This reduces puffiness, evens skin tone, and accelerates the skin's natural repair process — giving you that post-facial glow, daily.

Red Light Therapy

Collagen Stimulation

Red light at specific wavelengths is clinically shown to stimulate fibroblasts — the cells responsible for collagen production. Regular exposure reduces the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles and improves overall skin texture and radiance without any UV damage.

at-home face lifting devices

The market for at-home face lifting devices has grown significantly in recent years — and for good reason. Clinical treatments like microcurrent facials, EMS sculpting, and radiofrequency therapy deliver real, measurable results. What is EMS face lifting and how does it differ from microcurrent?

Both EMS and microcurrent use electrical current to improve the face — but they work at different levels. Microcurrent uses very low-level currents (measured in microamperes) that work at the skin and cellular level to stimulate collagen and ATP production. EMS uses slightly stronger currents to trigger visible muscle contractions — the kind you can feel — which physically tones and lifts the facial muscles over time.

The most effective at-home devices, including the Zential Pure Face Introducer, combine both mechanisms so you get cellular rejuvenation and muscle toning in a single treatment.

Who should use a face lifting device?

At-home EMS and microcurrent devices are suitable for most adults. They are particularly effective for people who notice early signs of facial sagging, loss of jawline definition, fine lines around the eyes and mouth, or general loss of skin firmness. Many users in their late 20s use these devices preventatively — maintaining muscle tone before visible aging begins.

How does an at-home device compare to clinic treatments?

Clinical EMS and microcurrent machines are more powerful, and a trained aesthetician can deliver a more targeted treatment. However, the advantage of an at-home device is consistency: daily use of a home device over 30-60 days typically delivers results comparable to a course of 4-6 clinic sessions — at a fraction of the cost. The key variable is regularity. A device used every day compounds; a clinic session once a month does not.

What results can I realistically expect?

Most users report an immediate lifting and contouring effect after their first session — particularly around the cheekbones and jawline. This is partly muscular (the muscles have been contracted and are temporarily more toned) and partly circulatory (increased blood flow brings a healthy glow). With consistent use over 4-8 weeks, the structural changes become more lasting: improved muscle tone, firmer skin texture, and reduced fine lines.

FAQ

Common questions

How quickly will I see results?
Many users notice an immediate lifting and sculpting effect after the first session — particularly around the jawline and cheekbones. Sustained, structural results become visible after 2-4 weeks of consistent daily use as the facial muscles develop tone and collagen production increases.
How often should I use it?
For best results, use the device for 10 minutes daily or every other day. Think of it as a workout routine for your facial muscles — consistency is what produces and maintains results. After an initial 4-week intensive period, many users move to 3-4 sessions per week for maintenance.
Is it safe for all skin types?
Yes. EMS and microcurrent technology is clinically tested and suitable for all skin types. The device should not be used if you are pregnant, have a pacemaker or metal implants in the face, have had skin cancer in the past 12 months, or have active severe acne or open wounds in the treatment area.
What do I use with the device?
You should always use a water-based, oil-free conductive gel or serum with EMS and microcurrent devices. The electrical current passes through water but is blocked by oils — so oil-based products will reduce effectiveness. Apply generously to the treatment area before each session.
Will results fade if I stop using it?
Like any form of muscle training, the results from facial EMS require maintenance. If you stop using the device entirely, muscle tone will gradually reduce over several weeks. Most users incorporate it into their ongoing skincare routine 3-4 times per week to maintain results long-term.
