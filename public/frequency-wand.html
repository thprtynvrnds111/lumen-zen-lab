Choose your wavelength

Different wavelengths of light trigger different biological responses in skin tissue. The Frequency Wand delivers three distinct modes — each targeting a specific concern.

Red Light

630–700 nm

Penetrates to the dermis where collagen is produced. Stimulates fibroblast activity — the cells responsible for rebuilding skin structure. Reduces the appearance of fine lines and improves skin density and elasticity over consistent use.

Blue Light

400–450 nm

Works at the surface level to target P. acnes bacteria — the primary driver of inflammatory acne. Blue light disrupts bacterial activity without antibiotics or irritants. Recommended for congested skin or active breakouts.

Green Light

520–560 nm

Targets melanin production and excess pigmentation. Green light calms overactive melanocytes — gradually evening skin tone, reducing hyperpigmentation and post-inflammatory marks left by breakouts or sun exposure.

How to use your Frequency Wand

01

Prep the Skin

Cleanse thoroughly and apply a water-based conductive gel or hyaluronic acid serum. Microcurrent travels through water — oil-based products block the current. Select your light mode based on your skin concern for the session.

02

Treat & Sculpt

Glide the wand along the jawline, cheekbones, forehead, and neck using slow upward motions. Pause on areas of concern for 10–15 seconds. The device's thermal warmth signals correct contact with the skin. Work each zone for 60–90 seconds.

03

Seal with Skincare

Apply your serum and moisturiser immediately post-treatment while skin permeability is elevated. Products absorb measurably deeper in this window. Vibration massage during application enhances penetration further.

Complete guide

Everything about frequency wands

Multi-technology facial wands represent the next generation of at-home skin care — moving beyond single-modality devices to address multiple layers of ageing simultaneously.

Who benefits most from a frequency wand?

Frequency wands are effective across a wide range of ages and skin concerns. They are particularly well-suited for people experiencing loss of facial contour, uneven skin tone, acne, fine lines, or general skin dullness. The multi-modal nature means a single device can address several concerns without switching tools.

How does a frequency wand differ from a standard microcurrent device?

A standard microcurrent device works on one mechanism — electrical stimulation of muscle and cellular tissue. A frequency wand adds colour light therapy, vibration, and thermal heat, enabling it to target the skin surface, dermis, and lymphatic system simultaneously. The compound effect produces more visible results across a broader range of skin concerns.

What results should I realistically expect?

Most users notice immediate improvements in skin radiance and slight lifting from the first session — this is due to increased circulation and muscle stimulation. Structural results (improved firmness, reduced fine lines, better contour) develop over 4–6 weeks of consistent daily use. Skin tone improvements from light therapy typically become visible after 3–4 weeks.

Common questions

Does the Frequency Wand actually work?

Yes. Each technology in the Frequency Wand has independent clinical evidence — microcurrent for muscle toning and cellular stimulation, red and blue light for collagen and acne, vibration for lymphatic drainage and circulation. Combined daily use compounds these effects. Results require consistency — like any form of training, the benefits build over time.

How is it different from the Face Introducer?

The Frequency Wand adds colour light therapy (red, blue, and green wavelengths) and vibration massage to the core microcurrent and thermal technologies. How often should I use it?

Daily use for the first 4–6 weeks delivers the most visible structural results. After that, 3–4 sessions per week maintains and continues to build on gains. No recovery time is needed between sessions — the technologies are non-invasive and suitable for daily use.

Is it safe for sensitive skin?

Yes — all four technologies are non-invasive and clinically tested for sensitive skin. Avoid use if pregnant, if you have a pacemaker or metal implants in the face, active severe acne breakouts directly under the device, or a history of skin cancer in the past 12 months.

What serum should I use with it?

Always use a water-based, oil-free conductive gel or serum for the microcurrent to conduct properly. Hyaluronic acid serums work particularly well — they act as an effective conductor while delivering additional hydration benefits during treatment. Apply your richer moisturiser after the session, not during.
