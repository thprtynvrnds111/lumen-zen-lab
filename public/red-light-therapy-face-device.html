The Mechanism

What Red Light at 630–660nm Does in Skin Tissue

The mechanism behind red light therapy is photobiomodulation — the ability of specific wavelengths of light to trigger biological responses in cells without heat damage. At 630–660nm, red light is absorbed by cytochrome c oxidase, a protein in the mitochondrial membrane responsible for cellular energy production.

When cytochrome c oxidase absorbs red light at 630–660nm, it increases the production of ATP — the primary energy currency of all cellular function. More ATP means more fuel for fibroblasts to produce collagen and elastin, for cells to repair and regenerate, and for the skin to maintain its structural integrity under daily stress.

This is not a cosmetic surface effect. It is a cellular energy mechanism — the same reason red light is studied in wound healing, muscle recovery, and neurological research. The skin application is one of the most studied and replicated effects in the photobiomodulation literature.
